DARLENE PÉREZ WIESNER

My career path was not a direct one. For more than a decade, I built a career in healthcare as a speech-language pathologist collaborating with patients, families and other healthcare professionals, to assess speech and swallow function in individuals with neurological disorders. I worked hard to help individuals improve their ability to eat, speak and function in their day-to-day lives again. While I did not know it at the time, this was the perfect place to hone the professional skills that I rely on and value today such as the discipline required to follow through and advocate for patients and families, the effective communication skills required to report to physicians what I was observing and what I was recommending, and the problem solving skills required to help patients manage their disorder and meet their goals.

It was when I was participating at the first Tech Moms in Color Cohort (TMiC) in the Fall of 2023, when I started to see a new path for myself -- a way to use my skill set of reporting, collaborating and communicating efficiently to identify and solve problems. During my time with TMiC, I received an introduction to the field of Quality Assurance and realized that my skills were inline with the skills required for this line of work.

Through the amazing community of women-tech professionals associated with Tech-Moms.org, I found my path forward and am making a major career change. I am preparing for work in an entry level position as a Quality Assurance Engineer. I am currently making plans to take additional course work to learn more about web development through the Utah Tech University (UT) Technology Development series in partnership with Pluralsight.
